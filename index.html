About Me

I am an Algorithm Engineer at Carl Zeiss X-Ray Microscopy, specializing in deep learning and computer vision. My work focuses on developing and optimizing self-supervised image/video super-resolution and denoising algorithms for applications in biomedical and semiconductor industries.

I have expertise in designing diffusion-based deep foundation models for X-ray reconstruction, leading Azure cloud-based development, and implementing LLM-empowered agents for various applications.

I hold a Master's degree in Computer Science and Engineering from Washington University in St. Louis with a perfect 4.0 GPA, and have published multiple papers in prestigious journals and conferences.
