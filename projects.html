Diffusion-based Deep Foundation Models for X-ray Reconstruction

Carl Zeiss X-Ray Microscopy | 2023-Present

Project Overview

Designed and developed fully-3D diffusion-based deep foundation models for high-quality X-ray reconstruction. These models significantly improve resolution and reduce noise in X-ray imaging applications for biomedical and semiconductor industries.

Technical Approach

  • Implemented a fully 3D architecture to maintain spatial continuity in volumetric data
  • Utilized diffusion probabilistic models to progressively denoise low-quality X-ray images
  • Incorporated domain-specific priors to enhance reconstruction quality
  • Optimized model architecture for deployment on high-performance GPU systems
  • Created efficient sampling strategies to balance quality and computation time

Results & Impact

The implemented foundation models delivered significant improvements in X-ray reconstruction quality:

  • 40% reduction in noise levels compared to traditional reconstruction methods
  • Enhanced spatial resolution up to 2x in challenging low-dose imaging scenarios
  • Reduced artifacts in limited-angle and sparse-view reconstruction tasks
  • Successfully deployed in production environments for biomedical research and semiconductor inspection

    • Developed adaptive dictionary learning techniques for sparse representation
    • Created efficient numerical optimization algorithms for large-scale reconstruction problems
    • Designed validation metrics for quantitative assessment of reconstruction quality
    • Implemented parallel computing techniques for accelerated processing

    Results & Impact

    The reconstruction framework achieved:

    • Superior performance in limited-angle scenarios compared to conventional methods
    • Effective recovery of structural details from highly incomplete projection data
    • Publication in IEEE Transactions on Radiation and Plasma Medical Sciences
    • Application to real-world CT imaging with reduced radiation dose
