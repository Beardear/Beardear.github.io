Assessment of deep-learning-based resolution recovery algorithm relative to imaging system resolution and feature size

Bukka, V.V.R., Xu, M., Andrew, M. and Andreyev, A. (2025)

Methods in Microscopy

This paper presents a comprehensive evaluation of our deep-learning algorithm for resolution recovery in X-ray imaging. We assessed performance across varying imaging system resolutions and feature sizes, establishing quantitative relationships between input image quality and algorithm effectiveness.

X-ray reconstruction using synthetic prior image restoration, with application to noise and artefact removal

Andrew, M., Andreyev, A., Yang, F., Xu, M. and Xu, S. (2024)

Developments in X-Ray Tomography XV (Vol. 13152, p. 131520E). SPIE

We introduced a novel approach using synthetic prior images for enhancing X-ray reconstruction. Our method significantly reduced noise and artifacts in tomographic images, improving visualization of fine structures and enhancing overall image quality for diagnostic and analytical purposes.

Self-supervised deep image restoration for x-ray computed laminographic tomography

Xu, S., Candell, S., Case, T., Goehnermeier, A., Irwin, J., Majlan, K., Preil, M., Ruoff, J., Xu, M., Yang, F. and Andrew, M. (2024)

Developments in X-Ray Tomography XV (Vol. 13152, p. 131520T). SPIE

This paper presents our self-supervised deep learning approach for image restoration specifically designed for x-ray computed laminographic tomography. The method eliminates the need for paired training data by leveraging the inherent structure of the imaging problem, achieving significant improvements in image quality for laminographic applications.

Progressive neural compression for adaptive image offloading under timing constraints

Wang, R., Liu, H., Qiu, J., Xu, M., Guérin, R. and Lu, C. (2023)

2023 IEEE Real-Time Systems Symposium (RTSS) (pp. 118-130). IEEE

We developed a progressive neural compression framework for adaptive image offloading under strict timing constraints. Our approach dynamically adjusts compression levels based on available bandwidth and deadline requirements, ensuring efficient transmission of visual data in resource-constrained environments.

Deep Iteration Assisted by Multi-level Obey-pixel Network Discriminator (DIAMOND) for Medical Image Recovery

Xu, M., Hu, D., Wu, W. and Wu, W. (2021)

arXiv preprint arXiv:2102.06102

This paper introduces DIAMOND, a novel approach combining deep iterative reconstruction with a multi-level pixel-aware discriminator network. Our method significantly improves medical image recovery quality by preserving fine details and anatomical structures while effectively suppressing noise and artifacts.

Limited-angle X-ray CT reconstruction using image gradient ℓ₀-norm with dictionary learning

Xu, M., Hu, D., Luo, F., Liu, F., Wang, S. and Wu, W. (2020)

IEEE Transactions on Radiation and Plasma Medical Sciences, 5(1), pp.78-87

We developed a novel reconstruction framework for limited-angle X-ray CT using image gradient ℓ₀-norm regularization combined with dictionary learning. Our approach effectively recovers structural details from highly incomplete projection data, outperforming conventional reconstruction methods in both quantitative metrics and visual quality.

SISTER: Spectral-image similarity-based tensor with enhanced-sparsity reconstruction for sparse-view multi-energy CT

Hu, D., Wu, W., Xu, M., Zhang, Y., Liu, J., Ge, R., Chen, Y., Luo, L. and Coatrieux, G. (2019)

IEEE Transactions on Computational Imaging, 6, pp.477-490

This paper presents SISTER, a novel tensor-based approach for multi-energy CT reconstruction from sparse-view projections. By exploiting spectral-image similarities and enhancing sparsity in the reconstruction process, our method achieves superior image quality and material differentiation compared to conventional approaches.

ℓ0DL: Joint Image Gradient ℓ0-norm with Dictionary Learning for Limited-angle CT

Xu, M., Hu, D. and Wu, W. (2019)

Proceedings of the 10th ACM International Conference on Bioinformatics, Computational Biology and Health Informatics (pp. 538-538)

This conference paper introduces ℓ0DL, a joint approach combining image gradient ℓ0-norm minimization with adaptive dictionary learning for limited-angle CT reconstruction. Our method effectively preserves edge information while suppressing artifacts, demonstrating significant improvements over existing techniques in challenging limited-angle scenarios.
